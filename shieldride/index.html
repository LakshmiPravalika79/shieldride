Adversarial Defense & Anti-Spoofing Strategy

A 500-partner syndicate drained a competitor's liquidity pool using GPS spoofing. Simple location checks are dead. Here is how ShieldRide fights back.

🚨

The attack

500 delivery partners organised via Telegram. Each installed a GPS spoofing app, pinned their location inside an active rain-triggered zone while sitting at home, and claimed simultaneously. 500 × ₹360 = ₹1,80,000 drained in one trigger event. The exploit: the platform only checked GPS coordinates, which are trivially fakeable at the app layer.

Layer 1 — Device signals that GPS spoofers cannot fake
SIGNAL 01

Mock location API flag

Android's Location.isFromMockProvider() returns TRUE when any GPS spoofing app is active. Hard binary signal at the OS level. Cannot be bypassed without rooting the phone — the vast majority of delivery partner devices are unrooted stock Android.

SIGNAL 02

Sensor fusion — IMU check

Real outdoor movement produces micro-vibrations in the accelerometer and gyroscope. A person sitting at home shows flat, near-zero readings. Spoofing apps move the GPS pin in software — they cannot move the phone's Inertial Measurement Unit sensors. GPS faked + accelerometer flat = flagged.

SIGNAL 03

Cell tower vs GPS mismatch

The phone's SIM communicates with a physical cell tower. That tower ID is cross-checked against the claimed GPS location. GPS says Koramangala. Cell tower says Whitefield. Spoofing apps move the pin in software — they cannot move the physical tower the SIM is registered to.

SIGNAL 04

GPS signal quality (HDOP)

Real outdoor GPS: satellite count ≥ 6, HDOP < 2.0. Spoofed GPS from app tools: HDOP = 0.0, satellites = 0 — the app synthesises the location without real satellite data. Suspiciously perfect signal quality = synthetic location.

Layer 2 — Signals that only appear in coordinated ring attacks
RING SIGNAL 01

Claim velocity surge

Genuine triggers: claims arrive organically over 15–30 minutes. Telegram-coordinated ring: 400–500 claims arrive in the same 60-second window because all members received the same message simultaneously. Rule: >50 claims from one zone in any 60-second window → entire batch held for review. Statistically impossible in genuine scenarios.

RING SIGNAL 02

Device fingerprint clustering

Fraud rings purchase devices in bulk and use the same spoofing app version. If 80+ partners in one trigger batch share identical OS version + device model + app version fingerprints — that cluster is held. Honest workers have organically varied personal device profiles built over months of use.

RING SIGNAL 03

Historical zone mismatch

Every partner has a 90-day delivery heatmap from completed orders. A partner whose entire history shows activity in Banjara Hills claiming a trigger in Kukatpally — a zone they have never worked in — is flagged. People do not travel to unfamiliar zones during active weather events.

RING SIGNAL 04

Velocity impossibility

GPS history scanned for physically impossible movement: Zone A at 2:00pm, Zone B 8km away at 2:03pm = 160 km/h on a two-wheeler. This is a GPS spoofing artifact — the fake app repositions the pin instantly when the user moves it manually, creating impossible velocity jumps.

A genuinely stranded delivery partner produces: mock location OFF, accelerometer data consistent with outdoor sheltering, cell tower matching their GPS zone, organic claim timing, unique personal device profile, and location inside their historical work zone.
